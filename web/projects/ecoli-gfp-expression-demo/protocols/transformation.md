# Chemical Transformation Protocol

## Objective

Introduce `pGLO` plasmid into E. coli K-12 HB101 and isolate transformant colonies.

## Materials

- Chemically competent E. coli HB101 (50 uL aliquots)
- Plasmid DNA (`pGLO`, 1-10 ng/uL)
- SOC medium
- LB agar + ampicillin (100 ug/mL)
- LB agar + ampicillin + L-arabinose (for induction screening)
- LB agar (no antibiotic) for control plating

## Equipment

- 42C heat block
- Ice bucket
- 37C shaker incubator
- 37C plate incubator

## Controls

- No-DNA transformation control (competent cells only)
- Positive transformation control (validated AmpR plasmid, optional)

## Steps

1. Thaw competent cells on ice for 10 minutes.
2. Add 2 uL plasmid DNA to 50 uL competent cells. Mix gently.
3. Incubate on ice for 30 minutes.
4. Heat shock at 42C for 45 seconds.
5. Return tubes to ice for 2 minutes.
6. Add 450 uL SOC and recover at 37C, 200 rpm for 60 minutes.
7. Plate 50-100 uL on LB-amp plates.
8. Optional: plate transformed culture on LB-amp + L-arabinose for induction screening.
9. Plate 100 uL of no-DNA control on LB-amp plate.
10. Incubate plates overnight at 37C.

## Expected Outcome

- Growth on LB-amp plates only for DNA-containing samples.
- Minimal or no colonies on no-DNA control plate.

## Troubleshooting

> Warning: If no colonies appear in DNA condition, verify antibiotic concentration and competence.
> Tip: If too many satellite colonies appear, shorten plate incubation or reduce plating volume.
